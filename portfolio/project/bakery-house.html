Bakery House

Role
Concept, Research, Visuals
Duration
May to August 2022

Project Overview

Bakery House is a traditional family-owned bakery located in downtown Seattle, dedicated to providing its customers with the highest quality ingredients, taste, and presentation. They offer a wide range of bakery items, from house-made loaves of bread, pastries, and cakes.

The Problem

Busy workers, commuters, and locals who lack the time or the ability to prepare delicious bakes.

The Goal

Design an app for Bakery House that allows users to easily and conveniently order and customize bakes for delivery or pick up.

The Design Process

Understanding The User

User Research

I conducted interviews and created empathy maps to understand the users I am designing for and their needs. A primary user group identified through research was working adults who don’t have time to visit an in-store bakery or who do not have to prepare and make baked goods.

This user group confirmed initial assumptions about Bakery House customers, but research also revealed that time was not the only factor limiting users from baking at home. Other user problems included obligations, interests, or challenges that make it challenging to get the baking ingredients or go to a bakery in person.

User Pain Points

Time

Working adults are too busy to spend time ordering and waiting in-store.

Options

Limited ready-made cake options in store and no ability to customize or personalize bakes.

Accessibility

Platforms for ordering food and bakery items are not equipped with assistive technologies.

Availability

Users often find that their favorite item is sold out when they arrive to order.

User Persona

Problem Statement

Katerina is a busy working mother who needs a faster and more convenient way to order a customized cake for a special occasion because ordering in-store takes too much time and isn’t a good use of Katerina’s time.

User Journey Map

Mapping Katerina’s user journey revealed how helpful it would be for users to have access to a dedicated Bakery House app.

Storyboard

Creating a storyboard for Katerina explores her experience with the bakery and reveals her flow of how she might interact with the bakery over time, providing a clear sense of what’s important to her.

Starting The Design

Paper Wireframes 

Taking the time to draft iterations of each screen of the app on paper ensured that the elements that made it to digital wireframes would be well-suited to address user pain points. For the home screen, I prioritized a quick and easy ordering process and provided a way for users to start a customized order to help them save time.

Digital Wireframes

As the initial design phase continued, I made sure to base screen designs on feedback and findings from the user research. This is the first wireframe that demonstrates the design thinking aligned with user research.

Providing users with the ability to customize and personalize bakes was a key user need to address in the designs. In addition to equipping the app to work with assistive technologies. The second wireframe demonstrates the design thinking aligned with user research.

Low-Fidelity Prototype

Using the completed set of digital wireframes, I created a low-fidelity prototype. The primary user flow I connected was creating and ordering a customized cake, so the prototype could be used in a usability study.

Usability Studies

Findings

I conducted two rounds of usability studies. Findings from the first study helped guide the designs from the wireframes to mockups. The second study used a high-fidelity prototype and revealed what aspects of the mockups needed refining.

Round 1 Findings

Users wanted better suggestions for what each interactive element represented.

Users wanted a way to view the prices of each item in the customization process.

Use accessible language (avoid jargon) when describing items in the customization process.

Round 2 Findings

The “Create Your Own Cake” button needed to be more prominent on the home page.

Users wanted to view the costs of the different cake sizes, making them aware of the final costs.

Refining The Design

Mockups

Early designs allowed for customization, but after the usability studies, I simplified the cake options and process by combing the cake tiers and size sections. I also revised the design so users could see the cost of the different cake sizes.

The second usability study revealed frustration with the “Create Your Own Cake” section, needing to be more prominent on the home page. To make the button stand out, I created a new card element with an image, price, and a call to action button. I also refined the layout removing the occasion filters and adding a “deliver to” section for users who want their bakes delivered.

Style Guide

High-Fidelity Prototype

The final high-fidelity prototype presented a cleaner user flows for creating and customizing a cake. It also met user needs for a pickup or delivery option as well as adding the ability to track their order.

Going Forward

Takeaways

Impact

The app makes users feel like Bakery House really thinks about how to meet their needs.
‍

What I Learned

While designing the Bakery House app, I learned that the first ideas for the app are only the beginning of the process. Conducting usability studies and peer feedback influenced each iteration of the app’s designs, improving the user experience.

Next Steps

Conduct another round of usability studies to validate whether the pain points users experienced have been effectively addressed and also to conduct more user research to determine any new areas of need.
